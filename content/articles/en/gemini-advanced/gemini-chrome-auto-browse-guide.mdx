## What Is Gemini in Chrome? — An AI Assistant Built Into Your Browser

In March 2026, Google significantly expanded Gemini in Chrome's availability, rolling it out to Canada, New Zealand, India, and more. The update also added support for over 50 new languages and introduced a preview of **Auto Browse** — a feature that lets AI automate browser tasks on your behalf.

Gemini in Chrome is an AI assistant integrated directly into Chrome's side panel. Beyond summarizing web pages, translating content, and generating text, the new Auto Browse feature enables Gemini to navigate websites and complete tasks automatically.

## The New Side Panel Experience

### Opening the Side Panel

Click the Gemini icon to the right of Chrome's address bar, or use a keyboard shortcut:

```
Windows/Linux: Ctrl + Shift + G
macOS: Cmd + Shift + G
```

### Google Apps Integration

The updated side panel offers deeper integration with Google's ecosystem:

- **Gmail integration**: Draft emails based on the content of the page you're viewing
- **Drive integration**: Export page information directly to Docs or Sheets
- **Calendar integration**: Detect event information on a page and add it to your calendar

### Image Editing with Nano Banana

The side panel also includes **Nano Banana**, an AI-powered image editing tool. Select any image on a webpage and apply edits — background removal, style transfer, and more — directly in the browser.

## Auto Browse — A New Paradigm for Task Automation

Auto Browse is the standout new feature in Gemini in Chrome. Simply describe what you want in natural language, and Gemini handles the browser navigation for you.

### What Auto Browse Can Do

```
User: "Compare prices for this product across 5 stores"
→ Gemini automatically visits each site and presents a price comparison

User: "Summarize news articles from the past week"
→ Browses multiple news sites automatically and generates summaries

User: "Fill in this form with my information"
→ Uses your Google Account data to auto-fill form fields
```

### How to Use Auto Browse

1. **Open the Gemini side panel**
2. **Type your task in natural language**: e.g., "Compare iPhone 16 Pro case prices on Amazon, Best Buy, and Walmart"
3. **Gemini presents a browsing plan**: You'll see which sites it plans to visit
4. **Approve and execute**: Gemini navigates the sites and compiles the results

### Current Limitations

Since Auto Browse is still in preview, there are some constraints:

- Limited functionality on sites requiring login
- May behave inconsistently on complex JavaScript-heavy SPAs
- Requires a Google AI Pro or Ultra subscription

## Configuration and Customization

### Recommended Settings

Customize Gemini in Chrome behavior through Chrome's settings:

```
chrome://settings/ai → Gemini settings

- Auto-show side panel: ON/OFF
- Auto Browse auto-approval: OFF (recommended — shows confirmation before actions)
- Language: English + additional languages as needed
- Data sharing scope: Page text only / Page text + images
```

### Privacy Considerations

Gemini in Chrome sends page content to Google's servers for processing. On sensitive pages (banking sites, internal portals, etc.), consider closing the side panel or temporarily disabling Gemini features.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Q1: Is Gemini in Chrome free to use?

Basic side panel features — summarization, translation, and Q&A — work with a free Gemini account. However, Auto Browse and advanced Nano Banana image editing require a Google AI Pro or Ultra subscription.

### Q2: Is Auto Browse available in my country?

As of March 2026, Auto Browse is rolling out as a preview, primarily available in the United States. Expansion to additional regions including Japan, Europe, and more is planned in future updates.

### Q3: How does Gemini in Chrome differ from ChatGPT's browser extension?

The key difference is that Gemini in Chrome is integrated natively into the browser, not installed as a third-party extension. This means better performance and seamless integration with the Google ecosystem — Gmail, Drive, Calendar, and more.

### Q4: Can I use the browser while Auto Browse is running?

While Auto Browse is active, it controls the target tab. You can use other tabs normally, but manually interacting with the tab Auto Browse is working in may interrupt the process.
