## Introduction

Taking meeting notes is one of those tasks that quietly eats into your day. You're trying to follow the conversation, jot down key points, and then spend another half hour afterward formatting everything into something shareable. It's tedious, repetitive, and frankly a perfect candidate for AI automation.

With Gemini, you can dramatically reduce the time spent on meeting documentation. By combining Google Meet's built-in transcription with Gemini's summarization capabilities, you can have structured, actionable meeting notes ready within minutes of a call ending.

This guide walks you through the entire process step by step — from enabling transcription in Google Meet to building a fully automated pipeline with Apps Script. If you already use Google Workspace, you can start putting these techniques into practice right away.

## Enable Automatic Transcription in Google Meet

The foundation of automated meeting notes is capturing what was said in text form. Google Meet includes a built-in transcription feature that records the conversation and saves it directly to Google Docs.

### How to Start Transcription

1. Join a Google Meet call
2. Click the "Activities" icon in the bottom-right corner
3. Select "Transcripts" and click "Start transcript"
4. All participants will be notified that transcription is active

Once the meeting ends, the transcript is automatically saved as a Google Doc in your Google Drive under the "Meet Recordings" folder. The organizer and the person who started the transcript will receive an email notification with a link.

### Things to Keep in Mind

- Transcription requires Google Workspace Business Standard or higher (it may not be available on free personal accounts)
- Accuracy depends on factors like speaker clarity, microphone quality, and background noise
- The feature supports multiple languages, though accuracy can vary compared to English

## Summarize Transcripts with Gemini in Google Docs

Once you have a transcript, Gemini can transform that raw text into a well-structured set of meeting notes. The Gemini integration in Google Docs makes this remarkably straightforward.

### Basic Usage

Open the transcript file in Google Docs and click the Gemini icon in the side panel. Try a prompt like this:

```
Summarize this meeting transcript in the following format:

1. Meeting overview (3 sentences max)
2. Key topics and decisions (bullet points)
3. Action items (with owner and deadline)
4. Items to follow up on in the next meeting
```

Gemini analyzes the document content and generates a summary matching your specified format. The output can be inserted directly into the document — no manual copy-paste required.

### Tips for Better Prompts

A few techniques can significantly improve the quality of your generated notes:

- **Specify the output format explicitly**: Tell Gemini exactly how you want the information structured — bullet points, numbered lists, or specific sections
- **Ask for filtering by importance**: Including "extract only significant decisions and skip casual conversation" helps Gemini focus on what matters
- **Provide role context**: Prompts like "summarize from the perspective of a project manager" produce notes tailored to specific needs

## Generate Meeting Notes Programmatically with the Gemini API

For more flexibility and automation, you can use the Gemini API directly. A Python script can process transcript text and produce standardized meeting notes at scale.

### Setup

Install the Google AI Python SDK:

```bash
pip install google-genai
```

### Basic Implementation

Here's a Python script that converts a transcript file into structured meeting notes:

```python
from google import genai

# Initialize the client
client = genai.Client(api_key="YOUR_GEMINI_API_KEY")

def generate_meeting_notes(transcript_text: str) -> str:
    """Generate structured meeting notes from transcript text."""

    prompt = f"""Below is a meeting transcript.
Convert it into structured meeting notes.

## Output Format
- Meeting title (inferred from content)
- Date and time
- Participants (inferred from speaker names)
- Summary by agenda item
- Decisions made
- Action items (with owner and deadline)
- Items for next meeting

## Transcript
{transcript_text}
"""

    response = client.models.generate_content(
        model="gemini-2.5-flash",
        contents=prompt
    )

    return response.text

# Usage example
with open("transcript.txt", "r", encoding="utf-8") as f:
    transcript = f.read()

notes = generate_meeting_notes(transcript)
print(notes)
# Expected output:
# ## Meeting Title: Q2 Product Roadmap Review
# ## Date: March 29, 2026, 2:00 PM - 3:00 PM
# ## Participants: Tanaka, Sato, Yamada
# ## Agenda Item 1: New Feature Release Schedule
#   - Beta release planned for mid-April
#   - QA team to be expanded by 2 members
# ...
```

### Generate Notes Directly from Audio Files

Gemini's multimodal capabilities allow you to skip the transcription step entirely and generate meeting notes directly from audio recordings. This works with Google Meet recordings, Zoom exports, or any standard audio file.

```python
import pathlib
from google import genai

client = genai.Client(api_key="YOUR_GEMINI_API_KEY")

def generate_notes_from_audio(audio_path: str) -> str:
    """Generate meeting notes directly from an audio file."""

    # Upload the audio file
    audio_file = client.files.upload(
        file=pathlib.Path(audio_path)
    )

    prompt = """This audio is a meeting recording.
Create structured meeting notes in the following format:

1. Meeting overview
2. Key points for each agenda item
3. Decisions made
4. Action items (include names of responsible people)
5. Homework items before the next meeting
"""

    response = client.models.generate_content(
        model="gemini-2.5-pro",
        contents=[prompt, audio_file]
    )

    return response.text

# Usage example
notes = generate_notes_from_audio("meeting_recording.mp3")
print(notes)
# Expected output:
# ## Meeting Overview
# Weekly marketing team standup. Discussed Q2 campaign
# progress and budget allocation.
# ...
```

## Fully Automate Note Generation with Apps Script

If you'd rather not run prompts manually each time, Google Apps Script lets you build a hands-free pipeline. The script monitors your Google Drive for new transcripts and automatically generates meeting notes using the Gemini API.

### Automation Script Example

```javascript
/**
 * Monitor the Meet Recordings folder in Google Drive
 * and auto-generate meeting notes from new transcripts.
 */
function processNewTranscripts() {
  const folderId = "YOUR_MEET_RECORDINGS_FOLDER_ID";
  const folder = DriveApp.getFolderById(folderId);
  const files = folder.getFilesByType(MimeType.GOOGLE_DOCS);

  // Process files created within the last 24 hours
  const oneDayAgo = new Date(Date.now() - 24 * 60 * 60 * 1000);

  while (files.hasNext()) {
    const file = files.next();
    if (file.getDateCreated() > oneDayAgo) {
      const doc = DocumentApp.openById(file.getId());
      const transcript = doc.getBody().getText();

      if (transcript.length > 100) {
        const summary = callGeminiApi(transcript);
        createMeetingNotesDoc(file.getName(), summary);
      }
    }
  }
}

/**
 * Call the Gemini API to generate a summary.
 */
function callGeminiApi(transcript) {
  const apiKey = PropertiesService
    .getScriptProperties()
    .getProperty("GEMINI_API_KEY");

  const url =
    "https://generativelanguage.googleapis.com/v1beta/models/"
    + "gemini-2.5-flash:generateContent?key=" + apiKey;

  const payload = {
    contents: [{
      parts: [{
        text: "Summarize the following meeting transcript "
          + "into structured notes. Include decisions "
          + "and action items.\n\n"
          + transcript
      }]
    }]
  };

  const options = {
    method: "post",
    contentType: "application/json",
    payload: JSON.stringify(payload),
    muteHttpExceptions: true
  };

  const response = UrlFetchApp.fetch(url, options);
  const json = JSON.parse(response.getContentText());
  return json.candidates[0].content.parts[0].text;
}

/**
 * Save the summarized meeting notes as a new Google Doc.
 */
function createMeetingNotesDoc(originalName, summary) {
  const title = "[Meeting Notes] " + originalName
    .replace("Transcript - ", "");
  const doc = DocumentApp.create(title);
  const body = doc.getBody();

  body.appendParagraph("Auto-Generated Meeting Notes")
    .setHeading(DocumentApp.ParagraphHeading.HEADING1);
  body.appendParagraph(
    "Generated: " + new Date().toLocaleString("en-US")
  );
  body.appendParagraph("---");
  body.appendParagraph(summary);

  // Share with team members
  doc.addEditor("team-lead@example.com");

  Logger.log("Meeting notes created: " + doc.getUrl());
}
```

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Is Google Meet transcription available for free?

Google Meet transcription is available on Google Workspace Business Standard, Business Plus, and Enterprise plans. Free personal accounts and Business Starter plans may have limited or no access to this feature. Check the official Google Workspace comparison page for the latest plan details.

### How accurate is the transcription?

For English, Google Meet's transcription is quite reliable in most professional settings. Accuracy depends on microphone quality, background noise, and speaker clarity. For other languages, results can vary. To improve accuracy, use an external microphone, speak clearly, and consider including a glossary of specialized terms in your Gemini summarization prompt.

### Can I use this with Zoom or Microsoft Teams?

Absolutely. While this guide focuses on Google Meet, you can download recordings or audio files from Zoom or Teams and pass them directly to the Gemini API. The "Generate Notes Directly from Audio Files" section in this article applies to any standard audio format.

### What about data privacy and confidentiality?

Gemini in Google Workspace operates under enterprise-grade data protection policies. Data from Workspace plan users is not used for Google's model training. If you're using the Gemini API through external scripts, review the API's terms of service and data handling policies. For sensitive meetings, establish internal guidelines about which recordings can be processed through AI services.
