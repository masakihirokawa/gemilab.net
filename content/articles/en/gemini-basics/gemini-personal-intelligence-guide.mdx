In January 2026, Google introduced **Gemini Personal Intelligence** — a feature that fundamentally changed what an AI assistant can do. On March 17, 2026, this capability expanded to all free Gemini users in the United States.

Personal Intelligence connects Gemini to your **Gmail, Google Photos, YouTube watch history, and Google Search history**, enabling it to provide personalized answers based on your own data. Instead of generic responses, Gemini can now understand your context: "When does my Amazon package arrive?" or "Find photos from my trip to Kyoto last month" are questions it can actually answer.

This guide covers everything you need to know about Personal Intelligence — how it works, how to set it up, real-world use cases, and how to stay in control of your privacy.

## What Is Gemini Personal Intelligence?

Personal Intelligence is a feature that allows Gemini to **access your personal Google data and generate contextually aware responses**. Rather than relying solely on general knowledge, Gemini can reference information from your connected apps to give you answers that are genuinely relevant to your life.

Google describes it as having two core strengths: **reasoning across complex sources** and **retrieving specific details** — and often combining both. For example, Gemini might connect a thread in your emails to a video you watched on YouTube to give you a more complete answer, without you ever having to specify where to look.

The apps you can connect include:

- **Gmail** — Flight confirmations, order receipts, meeting schedules
- **Google Photos** — Photo search, memory retrieval, album organization
- **YouTube** — Watch history-based content suggestions
- **Google Search** — Past searches informing future responses
- **Google Calendar** — Upcoming events and deadlines

For a broader introduction to Gemini, see [What is Gemini? A Beginner's Complete Guide](/en/articles/gemini-basics/what-is-gemini).

## Availability and Rollout

As of March 2026, Personal Intelligence is available in the **United States only**. Here's how the rollout has progressed:

- **January 14, 2026**: Feature announced and launched in beta for paid Google AI Pro and Google AI Ultra subscribers
- **March 17, 2026**: Expanded to all free Gemini users in the U.S., across the Gemini app, Gemini in Chrome, and AI Mode in Search

Google has indicated plans to expand to additional countries over time. Users outside the U.S. should watch for official announcements, as international availability will depend on privacy regulations in each region.

## How to Set Up Personal Intelligence

If you're a Gemini user in the United States, here's how to enable Personal Intelligence:

### In the Gemini App

1. **Open the Gemini app** on your phone or visit [gemini.google.com](https://gemini.google.com) in your browser
2. **Tap your profile icon** in the top-right corner and select **Settings**
3. **Find "Personal Intelligence"** in the settings menu
4. **Toggle on the apps** you want to connect — Gmail, Google Photos, YouTube, etc.
5. **Review the permissions** for each service and confirm

### In Gemini in Chrome

If you use Gemini through the Chrome browser extension:

1. Open Chrome and access Gemini from the sidebar or extension
2. Navigate to **Settings → Personal Intelligence**
3. Toggle on your preferred connected apps

### In Google Search AI Mode

1. Go to [google.com](https://google.com) and click **AI Mode** in the search bar
2. Access the personalization settings
3. Enable Personal Intelligence to allow Gemini to reference your data while answering search queries

## Privacy and Data Control

Google has built Personal Intelligence with privacy as a stated priority. Here's what you need to know:

**Opt-in only**
Personal Intelligence is disabled by default. Your data will never be accessed unless you explicitly turn it on.

**Not used for AI training**
Google states that your personal data accessed through Personal Intelligence is not used to directly train Gemini models. It is only referenced to generate responses for you.

**Granular control**
You don't have to connect all services. Connect only Gmail, or only Photos — whatever you're comfortable with. You can change these settings at any time.

**Easy to disable**
Turning off Personal Intelligence immediately stops Gemini from accessing your personal data. The toggle in Settings takes effect instantly.

## FAQ

### Q1. Do I need a paid plan to use Personal Intelligence?

No. As of March 17, 2026, Personal Intelligence is available to all free Gemini users in the United States. Previously, it was limited to Google AI Pro and Google AI Ultra subscribers. If you're outside the U.S., the feature is not yet available.

### Q2. Will Google use my personal data to train AI models?

Google states that data accessed through Personal Intelligence is not directly used to train Gemini models. It's used only to generate answers for your queries. That said, Google's privacy policies can change, so it's always a good idea to review the latest terms at [google.com/privacy](https://policies.google.com/privacy).

### Q3. Can I add or remove connected apps at any time?

Yes. Go to Gemini Settings → Personal Intelligence, and toggle each service on or off as you wish. There's no penalty for turning services off, and your data will no longer be accessed once you toggle them off.

### Q4. Is Personal Intelligence available in the Gemini API?

Not currently. Personal Intelligence is available only in the Gemini app, Gemini in Chrome, and AI Mode in Google Search. Developers building with the Gemini API do not have access to this feature as of March 2026. Google may expand API access in future updates.

### Q5. What if I share a Google account with family members?

Personal Intelligence accesses data tied to the logged-in Google account. If multiple people use the same account, all of their data (emails, photos, etc.) could potentially be referenced. For privacy, Google recommends using separate accounts, and Personal Intelligence is best suited for individual use.
