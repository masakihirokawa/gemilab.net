## The Basic Prompt Structure

The secret to generating good images is "precise, detailed instructions."

**Basic structure:**

```
A [subject] in [situation/background] drawn in [style], with [feature1], [feature2].
```

Let me explain with specific examples.

### Example 1: Beginner Level (Simple)

```
A yellow dog playing in a park on a bright sunny day.
```

This generates an image, but we can be more specific.

### Example 2: Intermediate Level (Detailed)

```
A yellow Labrador Retriever running energetically in a grassy park under blue skies, with a ball in its mouth.
Sunlight pouring down, warm spring afternoon atmosphere.
Photorealistic drawing style.
```

This approach significantly increases the chance of getting an image close to your vision.

## Key Prompt Elements

### 1. Subject (What to Draw)

This is most important. If it's vague, nothing works.

**Good examples:**
- Brown short-haired cat
- Woman in her 30s wearing a red turtleneck
- Old wooden single-family house

**Poor examples:**
- Person
- Something cute
- Nature

Be as specific as possible about your subject.

### 2. Background and Environment

The surroundings matter as much as the subject.

**Examples:**
- "Office desk" vs "Cafe" vs "Library" → Completely different images
- "Sunny" vs "Cloudy" vs "Night" → Totally different atmosphere
- "Urban" vs "Rural" vs "Space" → Vastly different backgrounds

### 3. Style and Atmosphere

The same subject becomes a different image depending on the drawing style.

**Types of styles:**
- Photorealistic
- Illustration style
- Oil painting style
- Watercolor style
- 3D CGI
- Anime style
- Comic style
- Sketch style

**Examples:**
- "Photorealistic and detailed"
- "Soft watercolor-like"
- "Studio Ghibli anime style"

### 4. Details and Features

Attention to detail increases the final quality.

**Examples:**
- Colors: "Warm tones," "Cool tones," "Pastel colors"
- Emotion: "Happy," "Calm," "Adventurous"
- Lighting: "Backlighting," "Spotlight," "Natural light"
- Angle: "Front-facing," "Overhead," "Low angle"

## Common Mistakes and Solutions

### Mistake 1: Vague Subject Specification

**Poor example:**
"A person sitting"

**Improved:**
"A woman in her 30s with dark hair, sitting on a sofa, wearing casual clothes."

### Mistake 2: No Style Specification

**Poor example:**
"Coffee on a cafe table"

**Improved:**
"Coffee on a cafe table. Warm-toned photographic style. Professional magazine cover finish."

### Mistake 3: Contradictory Instructions

**Poor example:**
"Dark nighttime scene with bright daytime landscape"

**Improved:**
"Nighttime cafe. Lit by warm interior lighting. Night cityscape visible through windows."

### Mistake 4: Using Negative Expressions

Image generation AI struggles with negative instructions.

**Poor example:**
"Doctor not giving injection. Patient not in pain."

**Improved:**
"Doctor warmly smiling at patient. Patient with reassured expression."

## Techniques: Tips for Better Image Generation

### Technique 1: Try Multiple Times with Different Prompts

Image generation actually involves an element of chance.

Running the same prompt multiple times sometimes yields surprisingly good images.

If you don't like the result, try several times.

### Technique 2: Specify "Reference Styles"

Using "like" expressions makes style specification easier.

**Examples:**
- "Like a Vogue magazine fashion photo"
- "Like a Studio Ghibli film"
- "Like an Instagram influencer's post"

AI excels at understanding "references."

### Technique 3: Specify Color Palette in Detail

The same subject with different colors creates completely different atmospheres.

**Examples:**
- "Warm palette, orange and gold tones"
- "Cool palette, blue and purple"
- "Monochrome, grayscale"
- "Pastel colors"

### Technique 4: Specify Emotion

Defining the subject's "feeling" establishes the image's atmosphere.

**Examples:**
- "Happy," "Sad," "Serious," "Relaxed"
- "Full of hope," "Discouraged," "Adventurous," "Quiet"

### Technique 5: Consider Size and Aspect Ratio

Different uses require different sizes.

- **Social media**: Square (1:1) or portrait (9:16)
- **Blogs**: Landscape (16:9)
- **Presentations**: Landscape (16:9)

Gemini usually allows size adjustment after generation.

## Practical Prompt Creation Flow

Here are the steps for creating a "good prompt."

### Step 1: Clarify Your Purpose

Be clear about "what the image is for."
- Social media post?
- Blog header?
- Presentation material?

### Step 2: Detail Your Subject

Be clear about "what you want to focus on."
- Person? Object? Landscape?
- Age, gender, clothing?
- Size and position?

### Step 3: Determine Background and Environment

Decide "where and when."
- Indoors? Outdoors?
- Daytime? Night?
- What season?

### Step 4: Determine Style and Color Palette

Decide "what atmosphere."
- Photorealistic? Illustrated?
- Warm tones? Cool tones?
- Bright? Dark?

### Step 5: Convert to Prompt Text and Execute

Apply to the template and complete your prompt.

### Step 6: Try Multiple Times and Select the Best

If unsatisfied, fine-tune the prompt and try again.

## Final Thoughts: Experimentation and Iteration

Gemini's image generation is deep and nuanced.

At first, you might think "that's not what I wanted."

But through trial and error—"when I changed this part of the prompt, I got this image"—you gradually approach your ideal image.

What matters is "careful instructions" and "iteration."

Please try it. You'll definitely be able to generate wonderful images.
